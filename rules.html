Twochre Rules

Rule 1: Preamble

Twochre Rules!

Rule 2: Player Count

Twochre requires five (5) players to play.

Rule 3: Decks

Divide a standard 52-card deck into two groups: the Player Deck containing the ace, nine, ten, joker, queen, and king of each suit, as well as the two of hearts (which is called the “Tooker”); and the Trump Deck containing twos (minus the tooker), threes, fives, sevens, eights.

Rule 4: Role Selection

Deal out the Player Deck one card at a time, one card to each player. When a player recieves the Tooker, they take the role of “Cat Herder” and the player to their left is the “Dealer”. Continue dealing until roles are chosen.

Rule 5: Dealing

To start a round, the Dealer shuffles the Player Deck and then deals five (5) cards to each player who is not the Cat Herder (including themselves). The cards must remain face-down on the playing surface in front of the players. In the end, the five (5) cards which the dealer still holds is called the “Kitty” and should be given to the cat herder face-down.

Rule 6: Strat Cards

After being dealt all five of their cards, each player (including the cat herder) picks a random card called the "Strat" which they are allowed to look at. Players may look at their own Strat card at any time but should keep the value of their Strat card secret. All other cards must always remain face-down on the table so that their values are unknown to all players, including their holder.

Rule 7: Cat Actions

After recieving their cards and selecting a Strat Card, the Cat Herder takes one of three actions: they can choose to take another strat card (called a “cat lasso”) which stays face up on the table so that all other players can see it; or they can also choose to exchange their strat card with another player (called a “cat burgle”), in which case the player from whom the strat has been taken must use their new card as their strat (and can look at the card); or they can not do either (or forget to), which is called a “cat nap”, in which case the player to the right of the Cat Herder will be the first to play a card during the round.

Rule 8: Trump Suit

At the beginning of a round, one card is flipped up from the trump deck, and the suit of that card is called the “trump suit” (or “trump”). This card must remain visible to all players throughout a round. If it is a joker, the cat herder chooses a trump suit after looking at his Strat cards but before taking a cat action. If the same trump is flipped twice in a row over two rounds, the dealer will become the cat herder before cards are dealt for the next round.

Rule 9: Stratopshere Rounds

The first player to act once a round has started is the player to the direct left of the dealer. They must put one card from their hand in the center of the playing surface, face-up and visible to all players. They can choose to play their strat card, in which case they must say “stratosphere” so that all other players can hear. In a stratosphere round, all players must play their strat card (including the cat herder, who can choose between their strat cards if they have cat lasso’d). If the leading player does not win the trick during a stratosphere round, they immediately lose one point.

Rule 10: Tricks

Each possible win during a round is called a “trick”. After the first trick, the player who won the previous trick becomes the new Leader and must put their card face-up in the center of the playing surface first. This proceeds until all players have used all of their cards (five tricks per round), at which point the round ends and the cards must be reshuffled and re-dealt by the player to the left of the previous round’s dealer, who is the new dealer. Play continues until a player has won by earning exactly four (4) points.

Rule 11: Score Tracking

All players start with zero (0) points. To win the game, a player needs exactly four (4) points. Earning more than four (4) points causes the player to "bust" and miss the winning value. For example, if a player has 3 points and earns 2, they will not win the game and will instead have 1 point.

Rule 12: Scoring Points

Points are acquired at the end of the round as follows: Winning two (2) tricks is worth one (1) point; winning four (4) tricks is worth two (2) points. Winning five (5) tricks is worth one half point (½). If every player wins one (1) trick, everyone loses one (1) point.

Rule 13: Winning a Trick

To win a trick, a player's card must be either the highest value played card of the Round Trump or of the Trick Trump. In all suits but the trump, the regular value of the card is the value of the card (with aces being high). In the trump suit, the jack of that suit is the highest card, and the jack of the other suit of the same color is the second highest card.

Rule 14: Tooker Scoring

Winning a trick having played the tooker will immediately give that player one (1) point, or one and a half (1½) points if they have a non-whole point value, or one half (½) point if they have a non-whole point value and earning another point would cause them to bust.

Rule 15: Rule Limits

There can only be twenty-two (22) rules in this ruleset. No rules can be added that would exceed 22 rules when made concise (in other words, keep it the same complexity as euchre).

Rule 16: Style and Font

All digital literature associated with this game (i.e. this ruleset) must be written in the Comic Sans MS font because people with dyslexia find it easier to read (and Tooker is for everyone).

Rule 17: Breaking a Tie

If two or more players acquire 4 points at the same time, those players will all return to two points.
